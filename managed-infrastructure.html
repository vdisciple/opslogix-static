Managed Infrastructure

  • Home
  • Managed Services

Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) is a type of cloud computing service where a provider offers virtualized computing resources over the internet. These resources can include servers, storage, networking, and other infrastructure components, which customers can use to run and manage their applications and data.

One of the primary benefits of IaaS is its scalability. Customers can quickly scale their infrastructure up or down to meet changing demands, without the need for purchasing and managing physical hardware. IaaS also offers flexibility in terms of payment, as customers only pay for the resources they use on a pay-as-you-go or subscription basis.

Compared to on-premises infrastructure, IaaS eliminates the need for customers to maintain and update their own hardware and software, which can reduce the cost and complexity of IT management. IaaS also offers greater geographic and organizational flexibility, as customers can access their resources from anywhere with an internet connection and can more easily collaborate with other organizations or teams. However, some organizations may have security or compliance concerns with moving their infrastructure to the cloud, and there may be additional costs associated with data transfer or integrating with existing on-premises systems.
